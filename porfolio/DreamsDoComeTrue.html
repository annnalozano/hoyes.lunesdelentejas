"Dreams Do Come True” is a project that delves into the meaning of considering a sick body in relation to aging and ugliness.

It starts from philosophy to understand the origins of the perception of ugliness as something intrinsic to any aesthetic, implying that any science of beauty is obliged to address the concept of ugliness as well.

In a later stage, biases of artificial intelligences are analyzed, and how they reinforce stereotypes and prejudices, contributing to discrimination and exclusion. The idea is emphasized that the possession of money can compensate for ugliness.

From the design perspective, the project’s scope extends beyond the human body to explore its presence in various facets of society.

As a result, a speculative scenario is presented that addresses the idea of gathering around a table, merging the creation of a performative event like a “Surgery Reveal.” Within this scenario, various pieces are generated that articulate a critical discourse about the influence of standardized aesthetic ideals, which can lead to a significant transgression in the appreciation of diversity and uniqueness. This transgression not only affects the self-esteem of those labeled as ‘ugly’ but also perpetuates a limited view of beauty and aesthetics.
